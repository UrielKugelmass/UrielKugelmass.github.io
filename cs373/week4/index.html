1. What did you do this past week?
This past week was a little bit special. On Wednesday it was Yom Kippur – The Jewish atonement day, which I explained in my last week’s post. For this holiday, we have to fast for 25 hours – no food or drinking allowed, so had to prepare accordingly. I started hydrating a few days in advance so that I would be ready for those tough hours, however, my main issue is always with food. I usually have low blood sugar so the last 4-5 hours of the fast were not easy for me. Luckily I could finish it and treated myself to some Queso from Kerbey Lane!
Apart from this I did regular schoolwork, worked out and practiced a little bit for my upcoming onsite interview. Took my first test of the semester and hanged out with some friends.

The overkill requirements were a pain; I will be honest with that. I am excited to get started with the bigger project and start working with my team.
5. What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?
My tip of the week is a plugin called Noisli. Sometimes it happens to me that I am working in the GDC or in a public space, and some people are having conversations close to me. This makes it really hard for me to focus on my work so I need a way to isolate my mind from what's going on next to me. I used to plug in my headphones and play music, but the songs' lyrics were also a distraction. But then I tried Noisli, which is a tool that lets you build your own "combos". These are combinations of natural sounds (rain, fire, thunderstorms, etc) that can be relaxing and help me be more productive.
